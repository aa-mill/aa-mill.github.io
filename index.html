< About Me />

I'm a first-year (G1) graduate student at Harvard University pursuing a Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics. I work in the Computational Science and Engineering Lab under the advisement of Dr. Petros Koumoutsakos. I graduated in May 2023 from The University of North Carolinia at Chapel Hill with majors in Applied Mathematics and Astrophysics and a minor in Statistics and Analytics. At UNC, I was advised by Dr. Joaquín Drut, with whom I developed the Classical Virial Expansion Engine (CVE²).

New Paper Coming
Calculating and Resumming the Classical Virial Expansion Using Automated Algebra
July 2023

Dr. Joaquín Drut and I have uploaded a preprint of our most recent work involving the Classical Virial Expansion Engine (CVE²). In it, we use schematic model potentials to exactly calculate the virial coefficients of a classical gas up to 6th order, and we use them (along with Padé-Borel resummation techniques) to investigate the convergence properties of the virial expansion of basic thermodynamic quantities.
