The stream function solution of the Stokes equation for steady flow with free stream velocity \(U\) past a sphere of radius \(a\) is

\[ \psi = \frac{1}{2} Ur^2\sin^2\theta\left(1 - \frac{3a}{2r} + \frac{a^3}{2r^3}\right). \]

However, this is only the leading order uniformly valid expansion of the stream function in the limit of small Reynolds number. What do the higher order expansions look like?

// Asymptotic Expansions of the Stream Function for the Flow Past a Sphere at Small Reynolds Numbers

I completed this project for Harvard's AM 201: Physical Mathematics course. It derives the matched asymptotic expansions of the stream function for low Reynolds number flow past a sphere, connecting the early works of Saul Kaplun & P. A. Lagerstrom and Ian Proudman & J. R. A. Pearson in 1957 to the formalism developed by Milton Van Dyke in his 1964 textbook.

# import libraries
import numpy as np
import cvee

# define system temperature
T = 1

# define interaction potential, parameterized by A, b1, b2
A, b1, b2 = 3, 4, 5
def potential(r, T):
    return -T*np.log(1 + A*np.exp(-b1*r**2) - (1 + A)*np.exp(-b2*r**2))

# specify desired virial coefficient orders
Ns = [2, 5, 9]

# get results
output = [cvee.Virial(N, potential, T).assemble() for N in Ns]

// Classical Virial Expansion Engine (CVE²)

The CVE² package computes the power series coefficients of the classical virial expansion of the grand-canonical partition function. Its development was the subject of my undergraduate honors thesis. After my defense, my thesis advisor and I extended the package further, and we published a journal article on our findings. Additional information can be found on the project's website.
